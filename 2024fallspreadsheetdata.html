Enlightenment & Revolution – Assessment Data

Franklin County High School • 10th Grade World History • 2024–2025 School Year

Teacher: Donald Wallace Total students with complete data: 103 Performance task: "How did Reason lead to Revolution?"

Class level outcomes

Summary
Across four World History sections, class averages on the pre assessment clustered in the mid 50s. On the first argument essay, they rose into the low to mid 70s. The overall average for all 103 students increased from about 55% to about 72%, a gain of roughly 17 points.

Pre assessment item snapshot

The table below summarizes the structure of the diagnostic pre assessment. Item level averages are not the focus of this packet, but are provided to show how the baseline measure was constructed.

Item type Number of items Focus Scoring detail
Multiple choice 8 Core Enlightenment concepts, such as natural rights, social contract, consent of the governed, separation of powers, and basic ideas about the purpose of government. One point per correct answer. These items provided a quick read on prior knowledge and misconceptions.
Matching 1 cluster Matching key thinkers (Locke, Montesquieu, Rousseau, Voltaire) to simplified descriptions of their ideas. Partial credit allowed for partially correct matches. Used mainly to see if students recognized names and core ideas.
Short constructed response 1 Students responded to a simplified Locke style prompt: Why might Enlightenment writers argue that people have the right to challenge their government? Scored on a simple 0–3 scale for clarity, use of the text, and basic explanation. This writing sample informed early grouping and the amount of modeling needed.

Note: Pre assessment scores were combined into a single percentage score for each student to serve as a baseline for growth.
